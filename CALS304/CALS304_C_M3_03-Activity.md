## Activity: Evaluating Regional Climate Goals with Smil's Transition Framework

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Introduction: Assessing Structural Feasibility

Ambitious renewable targets are common in contemporary climate policy.
What determines their success is not aspiration, but structure.

Large energy systems are complex, capital-intensive, and slow to change.
Historical transitions from coal to oil and from oil to natural gas
unfolded over multiple decades, even when technologies were commercially
viable (Smil, 2017). The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change
(IPCC) similarly notes that deep decarbonization requires rapid scaling
of infrastructure, electrification, storage, and transmission systems in
parallel (IPCC, 2022).

This activity applies Smil's transition framework to evaluate whether a
major regional decarbonization plan aligns with historical patterns and
current infrastructure realities.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Scenario: Structural Feasibility Review

The plan establishes two targets:

-   70% renewable electricity by 2030\
-   100% carbon-free electricity by 2040

Your task is to evaluate whether the structural prerequisites for
meeting these targets are in place.

Smil identifies four recurring constraints in major energy transitions:

1.  Slow capital stock turnover\
2.  Infrastructure expansion measured in decades\
3.  Supply chain limits\
4.  Political and economic durability

Recent IEA analysis emphasizes that renewable expansion without
corresponding grid investment leads to curtailment and reliability risks
(IEA, 2023). Use these principles to guide your evaluation.

# Synthesis

Energy transitions are material reorganizations of infrastructure. They
are governed by engineering limits, capital lifespans, supply chains,
and political durability.

Policy targets establish direction.\
Infrastructure determines pace.\
Bottlenecks determine limits.

A realistic assessment recognizes both the urgency of decarbonization
and the constraints embedded within physical systems.
